Personal Investment Thesis

A Guide Through Entrepreneurship

July 4, 2024

This is a note to self, born of personal experiences, advice, books, knowledge, and the mosaic of life's events I've encountered. The order of these thoughts does not prioritize one over another unless expressly stated. This is just a personal guide through my personal and professional entreprenurship journey, that constantly changes and adds more knowledge.

Have three to win, the fourth comes by itself.
Team . Idea . Timing . Money

The Founders

Brilliant thinking is uncommon, but courage is even rarer. Founders, the building block of a startup, are convinced they can change the future. Their focus should be on long-term goals, rather than rivals. Disruptors seek challenges but avoid unwinnable battles. Disruptors are people who look for trouble and find it, but don't pick a fight they cant win.

Great companies have secrets: Specific reasons for success that other people don't see. Every great business is built around a secret that's hidden from the outside. Each founder must sell, the idea. This creates a mafia type culture, which is intoxicating. It will attract talent, investors, and provide personal fulfillment to anyone involved. Pass on any company that founders dress up for a pitch, interview or meeting. Avoid social entrepreneurs that aim to combine doing good and making money, they usually don't end up doing neither. Doing something different is what's actually good for society, creating a business with profits to keep doing things different, and naturally better. How well does the founders know each other? Have they worked together before?

The Team

The foundations â€” a startup messed up from its foundations cannot ever be fixed. Bad decisions made early on hardly ever correct themselves. If founders develop irreconcilable differences, the company becomes the victim.

Everyone in the company has to get along, can the founder give a friendly hug to everyone in the company? if not, can he trust him? Its impossible to build a company without a team. Pay the CEO the least possible to have a sense of urgency, pay him enough for a long term decision making. Either set a max as a precedent to the rest, or humble the team by being paid the least. Don't fight the perk war. The founder should convince talent as a personal calling rather than payment, equity or perks. At the beginning everyone except lawyers and accountants must be full time. Period. At the beginning everyone comes every day to a physical office. Period.

Its more powerful yet more risky for the founders to be distinctive individuals. They should attract both fame and infamy. Be more tolerable with unusual, weird, nerd founders as long as they bring out the best work from everyone else in the company. Are these the right founders?

The Idea

Assess if the idea is vertical (innovative) or horizontal (improving existing concepts). Bold ideas are preferable to trivial ones. The idea should not only create value but also capture it. It must be difficult to substitute. The most valuable companies of the future empower humans not substitute them. Big data is usually dumb. Computers are tools not rivals. Computers find patterns that elude humans. Making something new is far more important than making something better. Technology is supposed to increase mastery over nature and reduce the role of chance in our lives, as humans we seek comfort in predictability. Do not limit yourself to technology.

Is the idea ready for the unpredictable future? Think about every angle, and how can it be applied in the future. (Reddit, FB, is the fuel for AI before it existed.) What's the secret behind this idea?

The Technology

Proprietary technology must be at least 10 times better than its closest existing substitute. Anything else is simply incremental improvement. Design improvements are hard to measure, but can make a 10x difference. (Apples proprietary tech is not 10x better than Lenovo, HP, Microsoft etc). Design = experience, which is extremely important.

There are two technical breakthrough approaches, technology of nature (a faster microchip or better code and secretes of people (human behavior, habits or approaches). The best place to look for technological breakthrough is where nobody else is looking. (AI wink, wink).

Does this company create a breakthrough in technology instead of a big improvement?

The Business Plan

A bad plan is better than no plan. Sales matter just as much as product. In a business money is either important or everything. A business is defined by its ability to generate cash flows in the future. Projections are useless and a waste of time for both founders and investors. At least until Series A. Tech companies will often lose money for the first few years, it takes time to build valuable things, wait 10 to 15 years. The only plan to be considered is the 15yr plan. Money makes money, follow exponential growth, compounding is the key.

Is now the right time to start this particular business?

The Market

Start small, really small, smaller than you think its small. Its way easier to reach a few thousands that really need a product than millions of "nice to have". Sequence and time new markets and scaling up a product. Dominate a niche and scale up from there, toward a long term vision. Competitive markets destroy profits. Can the idea become a monopoly withing its market? Entrepreneurs are always biased to understate the scale of competition, the fatal mistake of describing tour market so narrowly that you dominate by definition . Never lose sight of competitive reality. Trillion dollar markets mean ruthless, bloody competition. As you expand to adjacent markets avoid competition as much as possible. Will the market will be relevant in 15 years? Are the foundations relevant in 25 years?

The Distribution

Network effects make a product better as more people use it, but it has to be good so that the first users find value. Start in small markets.

Economies of Scale: the cost of adding one user is close to zero. Adding users does not require adding features.

Branding: Does it speak to its audience? Does it have the potential to be a monopoly brand? No technology company can be built by branding alone.

Advertising matters, and its not transparent and not the same as enterprise marketing. It takes hard work to make sales look easy. None of us want to be reminded when we are being sold.

Think about distribution as a key part of product design. Sometimes the product itself is a kind of distribution (Calendly calendar, Dropbox links) Customer Lifetime Value and Customer Acquisition cost are the temperature of your distribution. Hard to measure early on. Essential for fundraising and scaling. The bigger the deal the longer the sales cycle, and the opposite. Good sales enterprise starts small. The mission is to create a replicable sales/growth process.

Do you have a way to distribute the product? Can it be done within the nature of the product?

The Investors

A small handful of companies radically outperform others. One successful investment should equal the entire fund. If its not a 40x investment then perhaps its not worth it. Always focus on the substance of the business, not a diversified hedging strategy. If you think you are playing a lottery you will most likely loose. Problematic investments are a waste of time, resources and morale. If a company is going downward, leave it, don't go down with it. The most successful are obvious right away. Don't allow an investor to have an excuse to not fund you. Don't allow investors to make you fill like you own them, if you've actually put in the work. Don't ever feel bad for failing, investor took the risk of money, you took the risk of time. Once comes back the latter one does not.

Have clear ownership manuals of the company. Boards are amazing post seed round, the smaller the better, of 3.

What secret do the founders have that could perhaps share with you?

Customer

Customers wont care about any particular technology unless it solves a problem in a superior way. The world is not a laboratory, selling and delivering the product to the customer is as important as the product itself.

Luck?

Absolutely. Chance is underrated, planning is overrated. Discipline and consistency from the team will not trump luck, but it will help the chances.

You cannot change the world by planning, listening to advice or copying other peoples success. Treat the future as something defined by your product and advance to shape it.

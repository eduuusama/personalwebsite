How AI Could Transform the World for the Better

April 13, 2024

Staying open to ideas, embracing uniqueness, patience fuels the creativity's journey.

Let's start by noticing mundane and everyday acts, routines, and decisions. Although we don't choose when the idea will come, let's be open to possibilities and keep our minds open. Current proven solutions and products can sometimes be the least helpful; opportunities lie one degree away. Countless inventions were made by accident.

Understanding what makes you feel alive, let's embrace our weirdness and build our own niche. Addressing our uniqueness, let's comprehend who we are by reconnecting with our childhood obsessions (e.g., LEGO, art, building, technology, exercising). Ask your family.

Creating something worthwhile will take a lot of time, and we have to train for it, like a marathon. Preparation will be key; it requires mastering forms in our field, years of practice. Impatience is our enemy and the greatest obstacle to our creativity. The best route is learning for its own sake.

We want to create something quick and make an impact. But if not ready, customers will notice. The best route is learning for our own sake, starting with the customer experience and working backward. Asking ourselves:

Where can we take the customer?
How do we make him feel?
What are we solving?
Why will he stay?

When it comes to creative endeavors, time is always relative. Deadlines are extremely helpful but not necessary right now. Let's use our internal gut feeling and notice what really holds our interest. Working on multiple products and solutions at a time will prevent tunnel view. Always maintaining momentum and letting it compound, consistency and time will do the work.

Failures are necessary learning to get where we are going. Each one gets you closer. Enjoy the process of iterating. Leverage the power of being a little imperfect. Activate your network and seek mentorship for the fundamentals. Pause for a moment and grease up gears of forgotten skills.

Constantly remind ourselves of how little we truly know, and of how mysterious the world remains. Combine this with curiosity; always keep searching for answers and the pursuit of greatness. The work will reveal and make sense as we go.

Let's create a powerful underlying structure, something that will hold everything together. The idea will be ready when we feel it is. It will only get harder.

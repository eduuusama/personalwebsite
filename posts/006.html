How Can AI Startups Survive, Create Value and Possibly Win

The emergence of AI has sparked a surge of startups across various sectors, but only those that can truly innovate and reimagine human experiences will succeed.

Jun 2, 2024

The emergence of ChatGPT by OpenAi was the last drop that unleashed a tsunami of AI startups through basically all sectors, industries and domains. This groundbreaking innovation is poised to reshape every sector, from healthcare to finance, and the ripple effects are already being felt. Amidst the hype, a countless number of tools, startups, and APIs that have emerged, each fighting for a slice of the AI pie (which is a massive one). Yet, the harsh reality is that only a select few will succeed. To stand out and thrive, an AI company must be built on three foundational pillars.

Reimagining the human experience with AI

To revolutionize industries, and win the race in their particular sector, startups must go beyond merely integrating AI into existing frameworks. The true disruptors will be those who completely reimagine and redesign the human experience with AI. This means not just enhancing but fundamentally transforming workflows. Incumbents like Google, Microsoft, SAP, and many more, with established distribution channels can afford to incrementally integrate AI into their systems, creating a formidable barrier for new entrants. Incumbents that have distribution can push out incremental AI innovation into their existing workflows, blocking the opportunity for startups.

The key to overcoming this challenge lies in innovation. Re-inventing the user experience is essential in order to disrupt and wedge into these legacy situations. This means that for a startup to win, the focus needs to be on winning the distribution game and this can only happen by fully disrupting and fully re-imagining the existing workflows. Specifically, this means that instead of plugging AI into an existing UX (nobody likes the little bubble on the right of every screen), the startup must re-invent and make the entire UX better. A customers wont care about any particular technology unless it solves a problem in a superior way.

Creating a data well

"Data is the new oil" — has been said many, many times, and is specifically true now in the age of AI — ONLY if the data is: organized, accurate, relevant, safe, unique and consistent. If not we are back to square one, unless, we are creating data from a proprietary source and shaping it to feed an LLM correctly. Remember — big data is usually dumb.

If the AI company is purely algorithmic, without proprietary data set, then it has no moat and its not defensive long term. The value proposition comes from proprietary data that then can be fed into a proprietary ML (technology) to create a competitive advantage.

How does an AI company create proprietary data?

One way is through creating a new and valuable business workflow which generates proprietary data as result of running a workflow. These are not exactly considered AI businesses, but I believe they have the best advantage.

For example Company Y is the largest provider of fast casual photo and video services for e-commerce brands. Its core business involves virtual shoots, where brands send products to Company Y and receive high-quality photo and video assets. This process has generated millions of proprietary photos and videos. Now, Company Y has launched an AI offering that leverages this extensive dataset to accelerate and reduce the cost of creating branded assets. Proprietary data in, true value proposition out.

Bonus: The Technology

Proprietary technology must be at least 10 times better than its closest existing substitute. This is extremely difficult and anything else is simply incremental improvement. Design improvements are hard to measure, but can make a 10x difference. (Apples proprietary tech is not 10x better than Lenovo, HP, Microsoft etc). Design = experience, which is extremely important.

Technological breakthroughs often emerge from exploring uncharted territories. In the realm of AI, this means looking where others aren't. Whether it's developing faster microchips, creating more efficient code, or unlocking new insights into human behavior, the opportunities for innovation are boundless. The question every AI company must ask itself is whether it is creating a genuine technological breakthrough or merely an incremental improvement.

Does your startup have or is looking to create a breakthrough in technology instead of a big improvement?

In conclusion, the future of AI is bright, but only those who can reimagine human interaction, harness the true power of data, and develop superior technology will lead the charge. As a leader poised to build an AI company, the focus must be on creating transformative experiences, leveraging proprietary data, and pushing the boundaries of what's possible with AI. By doing so, we can not only ride the wave of AI innovation but also shape its course, bringing unparalleled value to customers and redefining industries along the way.

Reflections on the Last 5 Years of Entrepreneurship

Things I wish someone had told me, before building a business

March 30, 2024

In the ever-changing landscape of personal and professional development, certain core principles remain constant. Reflecting on my entrepreneurial journey of the past five years, I've gleaned invaluable insights. Through the highs and lows of startup life, I've learned valuable lessons that have shaped me for the better.

Be a Good Storyteller
Stories are the currency of human connection. They have the power to inspire, persuade, and unify. In the journey of entrepreneurship, storytelling isn't just about relaying facts; it's also about painting a vivid picture that resonates with others' aspirations and emotions. Audacious ideas motivate people.

Focus on the Frame
Perspective shapes our reality. When looking for a solution to a creative problem, pay attention to what's happening around you. Look for cues pointing to new methods or ways to further develop current ideas. Nothing is static. It's helpful to consider that there are always more options available to us than we realize. Nothing is as urgent as we believe it is.

Work Harder
Success is born from relentless effort and dedication. It's not just about putting in the hours but channeling our energy into meaningful pursuits. Regularly reassessing our work ethic reminds us that greatness is achieved through perseverance and grit.

Work with Great People
Surrounding ourselves with talented, driven individuals is not just a luxury but a strategic imperative. Great people elevate the collective performance, foster innovation, and create a culture of excellence. Regularly reassessing and cultivating relationships with exceptional colleagues and collaborators is key to achieving extraordinary results. It may also be one of the most rewarding things in life.

It's OK to Be Alone Because You Are Boring
Be ready for solitude. No one will understand you. Use it to your advantage as it offers a sanctuary for self-reflection and introspection. Despite the support of others, the weight of decision-making and responsibility can sometimes leave feeling lonely.

Rethink Health
In the pursuit of our goals, it's easy to neglect our well-being. Yet, without health, all achievements are hollow. Regularly reassessing our physical, mental, and emotional health ensures we have the vitality and resilience to pursue our ambitions sustainably.

You Can Delete More Stuff Than You Think
Concentrate your resources on a small number of high-conviction bets; this is easy to say but evidently hard to do.

Manage expectations to avoid disappointments. Outcomes are what count; don't let a good process excuse bad results.

Consistency usually will beat luck and risk.

Get back up and keep going.

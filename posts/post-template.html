How AI Could Transform the World for the Better

July 27, 2030

AI and its technological advances have increased life expectancy but damaged human wellbeing by making life unfulfilling and subjecting people to indignities. Modern society disrupts the "power process" - humans' natural need to work toward goals through autonomous effort. The AI system forces humans to behave unnaturally, restricts freedom and autonomy, and cannot be reformed, only overthrown. It will either survive and perfect human control, or collapse.

Modern life disrupts fundamental human needs by making survival too easy, removing meaningful goals, forcing dependence on large organizations, replacing real goals with "surrogate activities" like hobbies and careers focused on unnecessary advancement, and removing individual autonomy. AI creates an unwinnable trade-off: each advance seems beneficial in isolation, but collectively they restrict freedom. Once adopted, AI technologies become necessary as society reorganizes around them, forcing adoption. This process only moves toward more control.

Why Historical Certainty is a Mirage

How AI Could Transform the World for the Better

Mar 19, 2024

In the labyrinth of the human experience, history stands as a light shinning the twists and turns of our past. It serves both as guide and a cautionary tale. History teaches us to manage our expectations, to understand where we have lost our way, and to guide us in navigating the rough seas of life — giving us a false fantasy of relief. History is not a reliable guide for making precise predictions of impactful events.

Consider the most pivotal moments in human history: the discovery of Penicillin (1928), the peace keeping Manhattan Project (1945), the markets Black Monday (1987), the sudden collaps in the Dot-com Bubble (2000), the rise of the iPhone(2007), CRISPR-Cas9 Gene editing and modification (2012) — these are the outliers, the record-breaking events that defy prediction. The are the black swans of history, impossible to predict.

Realizing the future might not look anything like the past is a special kind of skill that is not generally looked highly upon by investors, inventors, founders, economist, you name it. The world is full of surprises. Things that moved the needle the most are things that history gives us little or no guide about. Unprecedented events, ones we cant be prepared to face. True for both scary events like war or recession, and great events like innovation and discovery. The further you look back, the less likely history applies to today.

In the internal struggle of risk and reward, there exists a gap — room for error. The gap between what could happen in the future and what must happen for success to be achieved is where we find true resilience. Endurance, an elusive quality, it the catalyst for compounding over time. Yet, in the pursuit of greatness, it exists a healthy dose of paranoia — attention and avoidance of too large of a risk.

For in the end, it is the unknown that truly moves the needle of human progress. It is the unprecedented events, the great leaps into the unknown, that shape the course of history. We stand on the uncertainty of tomorrow, let us take note of the lessons of history, but let us also embrace the uncertainty of the future.

This notion is simultaneously scary and empowering. It proves the potential for anyone to create the disruption or innovation capable of reshaping the world. Proving, the most unexpected origins often harbor the greatest the chances…

This that "never happened before" happens all the time. — Morgan Housel
